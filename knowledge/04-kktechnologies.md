## Core positioning

Kitty Kat Technologies is a business-first Data & AI consultancy.

We help companies move from business questions to practical AI, data,
analytics, and automation solutions that can be implemented, adopted,
and measured.

This Canvas is backed by people who understand both business operations
and the technical delivery needed to make Data & AI work.

## What we do

We combine domain expertise, process consulting, data engineering,
ML/AI architecture, and delivery coordination.

We find where value can be created or leakage reduced. Then we check
what is feasible with the client's data, systems, people, and processes.

The starting point is always the business objective, operating
constraint, or decision that needs to improve. Technology comes after
that.

Distinct from a pure tech vendor:
- We can sit on the client side and coordinate transformation while a
  vendor builds. We can also lead delivery directly. Either way, the
  work has to land in production and change something a manager actually
  decides on.

## How we work

- **Business-first, not tech-first.** Diagnostic, then sequencing, then
  selected delivery.
- **Domain expertise and technical delivery in one team.** We are not
  separate strategy and engineering shops handing each other slides.
- **From business question to implemented solution.** We close the loop.

## Selected track record

### 1. Strategic AI transformation for a major fuel retailer

220-station fuel retailer in Central Asia.

Work included AI readiness and roadmap design, owner-side coordination
of the data warehouse and BI foundation, customer segmentation, fraud
analytics, internal assistants, payment-process automation, logistics
automation, and early optimization products.

Business outcome:
- reporting cycles reduced from days to minutes
- internal data team established and operating independently
- first internal ML models already in production

### 2. Fuel supply optimization platform

Production fuel-operations software.

Work included stock tracking, shortage forecasting, procurement
recommendations, supplier ranking, purchase-order control, reserve and
redirect logic, distribution control, shipment timeline visibility, and
ERP-linked execution workflow.

Business outcome:
- procurement and execution control moved into one product
- shortage forecasting, distribution control, and ERP-linked shipment
  workflow now sit in the same operational system

### 3. Loyalty segmentation and reactivation

Fuel retail loyalty program with 380,000+ members.

Work included problem framing with marketing leadership, hybrid
segmentation design, deployment of 11 segments into the mobile app, and
campaign logic validated through A/B testing.

Business outcome:
- around 12,000 dormant customers reactivated
- segment logic adopted into the next marketing cycle

### 4. Fraud visibility and detection at full scale

Fuel retail loyalty program.

Work included analysis of 30+ million transactions, baseline fraud
measurement, scenario mapping, and design of an enhanced detection
layer combining rules and ML scoring.

Business outcome:
- fraud scope became visible and measurable
- tightened rules deployed
- ML scoring moved into rollout

### 5. Natural-language analytics for management

Large industrial enterprise with an enterprise data warehouse.

Work included semantic-layer design, KPI and business-logic grounding,
training-set preparation, and architecture support for a multi-agent
analytics assistant used by management.

Business outcome:
- more than 90 percent answer accuracy in production
- internal staff trained for ongoing stewardship

### 6. Dynamic pricing, customer intelligence, and churn analytics

Cross-sector work across mobility, D2C, and telecom.

Examples include:
- dynamic pricing architecture and ML prototype support for a major
  ride-hailing platform
- customer analytics, segmentation, and personalization for a D2C
  consumer brand
- telco customer analytics, tariff analysis, and churn modeling

### 7. Industrial and operational AI deployments

Cross-sector work in manufacturing, risk, logistics, and
testing/certification.

Examples include:
- ML-supported design optimization for industrial compressor units
- RAG assistants and CV-based crack detection in heavy manufacturing
- internal and technical assistants for a specialized risk consultancy
- AI-agent architecture simulating ISO audit workflows
